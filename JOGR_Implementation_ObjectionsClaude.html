Feel-Felt-Found Steps
Core Framework
The Feel-Felt-Found Method

A powerful approach to addressing any objection by building trust, providing social proof, and highlighting positive outcomes. Use this structure as the backbone of every rebuttal.

1
Feel

Acknowledge the customer's concern and demonstrate genuine empathy. Validate that their worry is reasonable and that you take it seriously.

2
Felt

Share that other clients — especially those in similar situations — have felt exactly the same way. Normalize the concern so they don't feel alone in having it.

3
Found

Explain what those customers ultimately discovered: the concern resolved, the outcome was positive, and the decision to move forward was the right one.

Example in Practice

Customer: "I'm worried the implementation will disrupt our business."

Feel: "I completely understand how you feel. Implementing a new system can seem overwhelming at first, especially when you're running a busy operation."

Felt: "Many of our clients initially felt the same way — especially those with complex operations like yours."

Found: "What they found, however, was that our phased implementation process allowed them to stay fully operational while transitioning to Odoo gradually. They were often surprised by how smooth the process actually turned out to be."

Cost-Related Objections
7
💬 "The implementation is too expensive." +
Root Concern
Budget constraints or perceived value of implementation services relative to their cost.
Rebuttal
Focus on the long-term return on investment (ROI) and cost savings that the implementation will provide. Odoo's subscription is approximately 7× lower than competitors' public prices — the implementation investment is often recovered within the first year through operational efficiency gains alone. Frame the cost as an investment with a measurable return, not an expense.
💬 "We can't justify the cost for a business of our size." +
Root Concern
Doubt that the implementation will provide sufficient ROI for a company at their current scale.
Rebuttal
Highlight the scalability of the solution and how implementation can be scoped and phased to match the business's size and budget. Encourage starting with just the essential 2–3 apps that solve their biggest pain points. It's easier to grow into the platform than to implement everything at once — and significantly less risky.
💬 "Why do we have to pay for the whole implementation before we go live?" +
Root Concern
Lack of trust or concern about paying upfront without seeing the full value delivered.
Rebuttal
Explain the upfront costs associated with project planning, resource allocation, and BSA assignment. Work begins immediately upon payment — not at Go-Live. Hours are consumed throughout the entire project lifecycle, from analysis through post-Go-Live support. The structure protects both sides by ensuring the BSA's time is committed and available for the project.
💬 "How can we trust that the implementation will stay on schedule and budget?" +
Root Concern
Worry about unforeseen delays, cost overruns, and fear of hidden costs or scope creep during the implementation process.
Rebuttal
Provide examples of past successful projects and emphasize Odoo's commitment to transparent communication and project management. Our 95%+ implementation success rate exists specifically because of the QuickStart methodology — phased delivery, a single BSA driving the project, and proactive scope management built into every step. We define clear deliverables upfront and surface issues early, not at Go-Live.
💬 "Why do we pay for user licenses during implementation?" +
Root Concern
Concern about incurring costs for licenses before the system is fully in use.
Rebuttal
User licenses are necessary for training and testing during the implementation phase. Users need live access to the system to be properly prepared for Go-Live — you can't train on a placeholder. Access during implementation ensures users are confident and ready on the day the system goes live, which directly reduces Go-Live issues and accelerates adoption.
💬 "You said the lead time is 3 weeks. Why do we pay before we get assigned to our BSA?" +
Root Concern
Concern about incurring costs during the lead time before active implementation begins.
Rebuttal
Clarify that the lead time refers to when active BSA-led work begins — but project planning and resource allocation start immediately upon payment. Payment initiates the queue for BSA assignment and ensures your project is formally scheduled. This is standard practice and protects the customer by securing a dedicated BSA for their project.
💬 "We expect to get refunds for hours we do not use." +
Root Concern
Concern about paying for unused services and not receiving fair value for the investment.
Rebuttal
All sales are final — there are no refunds, including timesheets. Explain how hours are allocated across the full project lifecycle: analysis, configuration, training, Go-Live support, and post-production. Having remaining hours after Go-Live is actually a benefit — they are available for the Second Deployment phase, additional training, or ongoing coaching. Unused hours are never truly wasted.

Timeline & Resource Objections
3
💬 "The implementation will take too long and disrupt our operations." +
Root Concern
Fear of prolonged downtime or resource strain during the transition to a new system.
Rebuttal
Emphasize the phased implementation approach: users continue in their current system while Odoo is configured alongside it. There is no "big bang" cutover. Each phase delivers working functionality in short cycles, minimizing disruption to daily operations. The business stays fully operational throughout the entire implementation.
💬 "We don't have the internal resources to manage this project." +
Root Concern
Lack of a dedicated team or internal expertise to support the transition alongside daily responsibilities.
Rebuttal
The BSA drives the entire implementation — the customer doesn't need a project management team. What they need is a committed SPoC who can act as the internal point of contact and champion. The BSA provides the planning, guidance, configuration, and training. The SPoC provides the business knowledge and internal authority. This is by design — we built the methodology specifically around companies that don't have dedicated IT departments.
💬 "Our business is seasonal and we can't risk downtime during peak periods." +
Root Concern
Concern about the timing of the implementation conflicting with peak business periods.
Rebuttal
The phased approach makes scheduling flexible. Work with the customer to map out their seasonal calendar and plan Go-Live dates around peak periods. Configuration and training can happen during slower periods; Go-Live can be timed precisely to avoid disruption. This is a planning conversation, not a blocker — and it's one we have with customers regularly.

Product & Process Objections
3
💬 "What if we need to integrate with another software?" +
Root Concern
Fear that their current software stack won't work well with Odoo.
Rebuttal
Discuss Odoo's native integration capabilities and available connectors. Note: Odoo does not build integrations for features already available natively in Odoo. For eCommerce, Shopify, WooCommerce, and Magento connectors are supported. For other integrations, explore whether the functionality already exists in Odoo before committing to custom connector development — most of the time, it does.
💬 "We've had a bad experience with ERP implementation in the past." +
Root Concern
Lingering mistrust from previous failed ERP implementations or painful transitions.
Rebuttal
Acknowledge their past experience directly and without dismissing it — bad ERP implementations are common, and their concern is valid. More than 50% of proprietary ERP implementations fail industry-wide. Then differentiate: Odoo's 95%+ success rate exists precisely because of the QuickStart methodology, which was developed to address the exact failures that plague traditional ERP projects. The phased approach, the single BSA model, and the commitment to staying standard are all direct responses to why ERP projects typically fail.
💬 "We're not sure Odoo can handle our industry-specific requirements, especially if we need developments." +
Root Concern
Doubt that Odoo is equipped to address unique or niche business processes, and concern with Odoo's development process.
Rebuttal
Showcase the flexibility and customization options of the solution and provide examples of successful implementations in their industry. The key distinction: there is a difference between customization (configuring Odoo's existing features to fit the business — unlimited) and custom development (building new functionality — used strategically). Most industry-specific needs can be addressed through smart configuration without touching code.

Support & Customization Objections
3
💬 "What if we need more customization than Odoo can handle?" +
Root Concern
Doubt about Odoo's flexibility and adaptability to unique business needs.
Rebuttal
Emphasize the platform's flexibility and customization options. The key distinction is between customization (configuring Odoo's existing tools — configuration, Studio, workflows) and custom development (writing new code). The former is unlimited. The latter is available when genuinely necessary. Most business needs that feel unique can be addressed through smart configuration, saving time, cost, and future maintenance overhead.
💬 "What if we need post-implementation support? Do we have to keep paying for hours?" +
Root Concern
Concern about being left without adequate help once the system is live.
Rebuttal
Odoo provides multiple layers of post-Go-Live support. Functional support (generic questions, bug reports) is available 24/5 by phone and email — free. For ongoing BSA involvement — expansion phases, additional training, new module implementation — additional success pack hours can be purchased at any time. Any unused hours from the original pack are also available for post-Go-Live use. You're never left on your own.
💬 "We want a BSA who is an expert in our industry." +
Root Concern
Desire for specialized industry knowledge to ensure the solution aligns with their specific business processes.
Rebuttal
Discuss Odoo's industry-agnostic approach and highlight that all BSAs have experience assessing industry-specific requirements and tailoring the system accordingly. Odoo's implementation team carries synthesized best practices from thousands of successful projects across every major industry — this collective knowledge is often more valuable than individual vertical experience. App Experts with deep functional knowledge are also available to peer-review critical decisions on any project.

Market Competition & Alternatives
2
💬 "Why should we go with internal Odoo implementation and not a 3rd party?" +
Root Concern
Doubt about whether direct Odoo implementation provides more value than working with an external partner.
Rebuttal
Working directly with Odoo provides a dedicated BSA for the project's full duration, direct access to the QuickStart methodology developed by Odoo itself, streamlined escalation paths, and a team whose sole focus is long-term customer success on the platform. Partners serve a vital purpose for specific use cases — but they are not necessary for this project's success. For standard implementations, working directly with Odoo is faster, more accountable, and more consistent.
💬 "Our business is pretty standard. I don't think we need implementation. We'll do it ourselves." +
Root Concern
Underestimation of implementation complexity and the expertise required for optimal configuration and user adoption.
Rebuttal
Self-implementations often start well and stall. The complexity isn't in the software — Odoo is genuinely easy to use. The complexity is in mapping business processes to the right Odoo features, managing change across the organization, migrating data cleanly, and ensuring users actually adopt the system. These are the areas where expert guidance delivers the most value and where self-implementations most commonly get stuck — often months in, after significant time has already been lost.

The modular architecture means the customer starts with what they need now and expands into what they need later — without migrating to a new platform, rebuilding from scratch, or facing a painful re-implementation.

Change Management
2
💬 "Our team isn't ready for such a big change. We're worried our staff won't adopt the new system." +
Root Concern
Resistance to change due to fear of the unknown or internal pushback and concerns about employee buy-in.
Rebuttal
Change management is a core part of Odoo's implementation methodology — not an afterthought. The phased approach, the SPoC champion model, the executive sponsor role, and hands-on training are all specifically designed to drive adoption. Users who are involved early, trained on features that directly help them, and supported by their own internal champion adopt the system far more successfully than those subjected to a one-time launch event.
💬 "Odoo is too complex for our team to use effectively." +
Root Concern
Concerns about the learning curve and whether staff will be able to use the system effectively day-to-day.
Rebuttal
Highlight the user-friendly interface and the availability of training and support to facilitate user adoption. Odoo is significantly simpler than traditional ERPs — this is one of its core design principles. The interface is consumer-grade in its clarity. More than 18% of SMEs have never deployed integrated management software because traditional ERPs are "too complex and too expensive." Odoo was built to solve that exact problem. The BSA's role is to configure the system to match how the team actually works.
