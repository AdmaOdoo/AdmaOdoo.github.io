Tab 01
Must-Have Requirements

Every project must meet these requirements before it is sold. Check off each item as you qualify the deal. Items marked in red are non-negotiable hard stops.

🚨
Non-Compliance Consequence: If a project does not meet these guidelines, the BSA Manager presents the project and its issues to the Sales Manager. The Sales Manager takes over until the project is either refunded or restarted with expectations fully aligned. There are no exceptions.
Project Qualification Checklist
Project meets minimum requirements displayed on the Pricing Page — check apps per pack type and type of service per pack type. MUST
Account Manager knows the pricing page's recommended project hours and proposes the minimum to the customer. MUST
Packs of 25 hours or less are sold with the explicit understanding that they are typically insufficient to complete a full implementation. WARN
Sales has detailed a clear scope of what will be completed with the purchased hours — preventing scope creep and underselling. First phase = as-is essential flows. Further phases = to-be's and nice-to-haves. MUST
Customer is hosted on Odoo.sh or SaaS. This applies to all packs without exception. MUST
Odoo oversees the entire project: planning, analyzing, training the SPoC, configuring, going live, supporting post Go-Live, and developments. (Note: only training and limited configuring apply to 4-hour packs.) MUST
At the time of sale, there are NO formal plans to transfer to a partner or third party after Odoo's work is complete. Planning a transfer from day one is a disservice to the customer. STOP
SPoC is identified and committed. Their required time increases in relation to BSA hours — if BSA dedicates 5 hrs, SPoC may dedicate 10 hrs. SPoC must be prepared to: learn Odoo, promote it internally, test BSA-provided flows, plan & execute end-user training, create user manuals/documentation, and work on all open BSA-assigned tasks. MUST
No integrations to other software for features Odoo already has. Exceptions only: Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento connectors, and SPS Commerce. STOP
If a development is discussed or sold, the "Development FAQ" has been sent to the customer to align on Odoo's internal way of working and properly manage expectations. MUST
If custom development or advanced Studio use is anticipated, the client is on a major Odoo version or Odoo.sh. Custom developments are only deliverable on Odoo.SH. MUST
In case of conflicting information from different client employees, the BSA will carry out only what the SPoC has defined as a must-have. The SPoC's role: gather stakeholders, challenge requests, define priorities, and advocate Odoo standard features. MUST
All sales are final. There are no refunds (which includes timesheets). Customer has been informed. STOP
Current lead time has been communicated to the customer. If the customer goes on hold or abandons the project for 1+ month, they re-enter the queue at current lead time. Any transition time will be billed to the customer. WARN

Support-Only Success Packs

Success packs can also be sold for the sole purpose of dedicated support. In this case, it is not a QuickStart project — Odoo exclusively reacts to customer questions and does not drive the implementation. Make sure this distinction is clear to both the customer and the BSA at the time of sale.

Tab 02
Case-by-Case Review

These scenarios require review before the project is sold. They are not automatic disqualifiers, but they must be flagged, reviewed, and approved through the correct process.

⚠️
Important: These cases require escalation and approval — they cannot be resolved by the AE alone. Follow the review process outlined for each case before committing anything to the customer.
1
Development exceeds 20% of total project hours
+

A project must maintain at least an 80% standard / 20% development time ratio. A 100-hour project should not include more than 20 hours of custom development.

✓ 80% Standard Work
20% Dev Max
Review Process
  • AE should attach a screenshot of an email to the client in the success pack task
  • Email must explain that the client purchased less than the recommended hours
  • This documentation protects both parties and ensures expectations are aligned
📋 Document in success pack task
2
Development LoC maintenance exceeds 50% of subscription MRR
+

If the lines-of-code (LoC) maintenance cost for custom development exceeds 50% of the customer's subscription MRR, the project requires case-by-case review.

Scope of Grandfathering
  • Grandfathering may be considered within reason
  • Front-end eCommerce developments are not within reason for grandfathering
  • Connector developments are not within reason for grandfathering
Review Process
  • Follow the instructions in the Development Services document
  • Do not commit to the customer before review is complete
📋 Follow Development Services process
3
Client requests historical data import before Go-Live
+

If a client requests historical data to be imported into Odoo before going live, it must be reviewed by the Pre-Sales team or BSA Managers before being promised.

What Is NOT Considered Historical Data
  • Open invoices and bills for accounting go-live
  • Open bills for accounting go-live
  • Trial balances for accounting go-live
What IS Considered Historical Data
  • Closed accounting data
  • Closed invoices
  • Order history
  • Any other closed records — these are difficult to import and may require development
Review Process
  • AE should ping MAC, SLO, or APT for approval on the pre-sale ticket
  • Do not promise historical data import without Pre-Sales or BSA Manager sign-off
📋 Ping MAC / SLO / APT on presale ticket
Tab 03
Success Pack vs. Partner Pack

Two distinct service models with fundamentally different responsibilities. Confusing them during a sale creates real problems downstream. Know the difference before any customer conversation.

✦ Success Pack (Direct Odoo) ◈ Partner Pack
Odoo leads the implementation and is fully responsible for project success Partner leads the implementation; Odoo is not responsible for project success
Proactive — BSA defines an implementation plan and follows up regularly with the customer throughout the project Reactive — Odoo answers when the partner contacts them; no implementation planning is provided by Odoo
QuickStart methodology applied consistently to every project No specific methodology — partner follows their own approach (OIM mindset is recommended)
Can say no to a dev request. Client must pay LoC maintenance for all developments. Cannot decline dev requests (except certain third-party integration requirements). No maintenance fee — but partner is responsible for all bug fixes and code upgrades.
Dedicated BSA assigned to the project for its full duration Different BSA assigned per request via ticketing system (partnerus@mail.odoo.com)
SaaS and Odoo.SH implementations SaaS, Odoo.SH, and limited support for On-Premise implementations
Expires in 1 year Does not expire
Sold by DS and CS to end-customers, or by PR & AM when a partner is shadowing the implementation for learning purposes Sold by AM or PR to official and ready partners only
💡
Partners serve a vital purpose for specific use cases — but they are not necessary for most projects. For standard QuickStart implementations, working directly with Odoo via a Success Pack is faster, more accountable, and more consistent. Lead with the Success Pack when it's the right fit — which it usually is.

Key Principle

Odoo SH with a client-owned repository requires a very technical client or a handoff to a partner. Always confirm hosting type and technical capability before committing to any third-party app support.
